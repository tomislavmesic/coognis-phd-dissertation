# Phase 1 Questionnaire - Hidden Knowledge Within the Organization

This directory contains the participant-facing questionnaire instrument used in Phase 1 of the dissertation methodology.

The questionnaire supports the dissertation topic:

**Enhancing Internal Knowledge Discovery through Artificial Intelligence and Psychological Profiling**

## Study Objective

The objective of the questionnaire is to examine organizational awareness of hidden knowledge, willingness to share knowledge, the perceived role of personality in organizational processes, and willingness to apply artificial intelligence within everyday organizational work.

The questionnaire provides the empirical basis for the Phase 1 behavioral economics and organizational analysis. The cleaned and anonymized respondent-level dataset derived from this instrument is stored in `../data/`.

## Questionnaire Design

The instrument is a structured survey questionnaire containing 28 items. It combines categorical, ordinal, multiple-choice, and optional open-text questions.

The survey is organized into four thematic parts:

| Part | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| Part 1 | Knowledge management and willingness to share knowledge within the organization. |
| Part 2 | Influence of personality types on organizational processes. |
| Part 3 | Willingness to apply artificial intelligence within the organization. |
| Part 4 | General information about research participants. |

## Thematic Structure

Part 1 examines whether formal organizational processes, knowledge bases, expert-finding systems, and project knowledge systems exist and are used. It also captures awareness of hidden knowledge, perceived utilization of internal knowledge, perceived economic value, fear of knowledge sharing, and willingness to share knowledge with colleagues and management.

Part 2 examines personality-related preferences in organizational collaboration and willingness to participate in personality assessment.

Part 3 examines acceptance of artificial intelligence for work support, personality-profile use, internal communication analysis, public-profile analysis, and work-content analysis.

Part 4 captures demographic and organizational context variables, including gender, age group, organization size, tenure, education level, decision-making level, and optional e-mail contact for receiving research results.

## Survey Workflow

Participants first read the opening screen and study description. They then complete the four questionnaire parts in sequence. The instrument is anonymous, and the optional e-mail field is separated from the analyzable research variables.
