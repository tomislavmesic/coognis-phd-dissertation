# Opening Screen

## Study Title

Hidden Knowledge Within the Organization

## Introduction

Without exception, in every organization, regardless of its maturity and size, there is a certain amount of hidden knowledge. Certain activities are often repeated without awareness that someone has already completed or resolved a similar task. Sometimes we do not have the knowledge needed to solve a task, while colleagues nearby may have exactly the knowledge we are searching for.

This survey questionnaire contains 28 questions created for academic research in the domain of behavioral economics. It focuses on incentives for sharing knowledge within organizations, awareness of hidden knowledge, and possibilities for addressing this problem.

The first part of the questionnaire examines awareness of the existence of hidden knowledge, its scale, potential, and influence, as well as willingness to share one's own knowledge. The second part examines the influence of personality on internal processes in organizations. The third part examines willingness to accept artificial intelligence (AI) in everyday work in order to achieve better personal, organizational, and business results.

It is enough to complete the questionnaire only once. The expected completion time is up to 10 minutes.

Participation in the research is anonymous. Respondents are not asked to enter any information that would identify them.

At the end of the survey, respondents may optionally leave an e-mail address if they wish to receive the research results.

The survey questionnaire was available until September 15, 2023.
