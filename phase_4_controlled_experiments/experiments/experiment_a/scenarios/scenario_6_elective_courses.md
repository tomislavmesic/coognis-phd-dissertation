# Choosing Between Two Courses

## Scenario Context

You must choose one elective course for the next semester: Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals or Digital Marketing Analytics.
You ask the university support system for advice on how to decide.

## User Prompt

> I must pick one elective: AI Fundamentals or Digital Marketing Analytics. Which should I choose?

## Generic Response

When choosing between two courses, it is generally recommended to evaluate several relevant factors. Students should review the course content and consider their personal interests, expected workload, and alignment with academic goals. If additional clarification is needed, contacting the course instructors may provide further information to support the decision.

## Adapted Responses

### STJ

To choose between the two courses, follow a structured comparison process.
1. Review the content of both courses.
2. Compare how each course aligns with your interests.
3. Evaluate the expected workload for each option.
4. Consider how each course fits your academic goals.
5. If needed, contact the instructors for additional details.

### NTP

Choosing between two courses can be approached as a comparison of key decision factors.

The course content defines what you will learn, while personal interest affects motivation and engagement. Workload determines the required time and effort, and alignment with academic goals ensures long-term relevance. By analyzing these factors, students can make a more informed decision. If necessary, instructors can provide additional clarification.

### NFJ

It is normal to feel unsure when choosing between courses, especially when both options seem relevant.

A helpful approach is to look at the course content and think about which topics interest you more. It is also useful to consider the workload and how each course fits your academic goals. If you need more clarity, reaching out to the instructors can help you feel more confident in your decision.

### SFP

- Compare the course content.
- Choose the one that matches your interests.
- Check the workload.
- Make sure it fits your academic goals.
- Contact instructors if needed
