# Attendance Policy

## Scenario Context

You are enrolled in a course at the university. Due to personal circumstances, you may miss several classes.
You ask the university support system what will happen if you exceed the allowed number of absences.

## User Prompt

> What happens if I miss more than the allowed number of classes?

## Generic Response

Attendance requirements are typically defined at the course level and may vary depending on the specific subject. In most cases, courses establish a maximum number of allowed absences. Exceeding this limit may have consequences related to course completion or eligibility for assessment. Students are advised to review the course syllabus or consult the instructor for detailed information.

## Adapted Responses

### STJ

If you want to understand the attendance policy, follow these steps to get accurate information.
1. Check the course syllabus for attendance requirements.
2. Identify the maximum number of allowed absences.
3. Be aware that exceeding this limit may affect course completion or exam eligibility.
4. If anything is unclear, contact the course instructor.

### NTP

Attendance policies are defined individually for each course, which means the exact rules depend on the specific subject.

Most courses set a maximum number of allowed absences. If this limit is exceeded, it can impact course completion or eligibility for exams. To determine the exact threshold, the student should review the course syllabus or contact the instructor for clarification.

### NFJ

Each course defines its own attendance requirements, including how many absences are allowed. Exceeding that limit may affect your ability to complete the course or take the exam. To be sure, check the course syllabus or contact your instructor, who can provide clear guidance.
