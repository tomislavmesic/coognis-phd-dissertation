# Suspicious Email

## Scenario Context

While checking your university email, you receive a message asking you to confirm your password through a link. Because the message looks suspicious, you ask the university IT support system what you should do.

## User Prompt

> I received an email asking for my password. It looks official. What should I do?

## Generic Response

Emails requesting confirmation of passwords through links may indicate a potential phishing attempt. It is recommended not to interact with such messages or provide login credentials. The message should be reported to the appropriate IT or security service and then removed from the inbox. If any interaction with the link has already occurred, changing the password is advised.

## Adapted Responses

### STJ

If you receive a suspicious email asking for your password, follow the standard security procedure.
1. Do not click the link.
2. Do not enter your login credentials.
3. Forward or report the email to IT or the security team.
4. Delete the email from your inbox.
5. If you clicked the link, change your password immediately.

### NTP

Emails that request password confirmation through links are a common form of phishing attack.

The link is typically used to capture login credentials, which is why it is important not to click it or enter any information. The correct approach is to report the email to IT or the security team so it can be analyzed. After reporting, the message should be deleted. If the link was already opened, changing the password helps prevent unauthorized access.

### NFJ

You did the right thing by being cautious, as messages like this can sometimes be unsafe.

Avoid clicking the link or entering your login details. It is best to report the email to IT or the security team so they can check it. After that, you can delete the message. If you have already clicked the link, changing your password is a safe precaution.

### SFP

- Do not click the link.
- Do not enter your credentials.
- Report the email to IT or security.
- Delete the email.
- Change your password if you clicked the link.
