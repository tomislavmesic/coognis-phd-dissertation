# Course Schedule Conflict

## Scenario Context

You, as a user, notice that two compulsory courses are scheduled at the same time in your timetable.
Because both courses are required, you contact the university support system to understand how to resolve the conflict.
The system connects you with a study advisor.

## Core Facts

- Contact the study office
- Submit a schedule conflict request
- Provide course details
- Wait for review
- Follow the assigned resolution

## Matched Interaction

Each participant evaluates the matched condition corresponding to their selected communication persona.

### STJ User -> STJ Expert

> **User:**
> Two of my compulsory courses are scheduled at the same time. What should I do?

> **Expert:**
> Follow the standard procedure for resolving course schedule conflicts.
>
> Step 1: Contact the study office to report the conflict.
> Step 2: Submit a schedule conflict request.

> **User:**
> How do I submit the request?

> **Expert:**
> Step 3: Provide the required course details when submitting the request.

> **User:**
> What happens after I submit it?

> **Expert:**
> Step 4: Wait while the study office reviews the request.

> **User:**
> Will I be informed about the solution?

> **Expert:**
> Step 5: Follow the assigned resolution once the study office provides the outcome.

### NTP User -> NTP Expert

> **User:**
> Two of my compulsory courses are scheduled at the same time. What should I do?

> **Expert:**
> When two required courses overlap, the university needs to formally evaluate the conflict before assigning a solution.
> Because of that, the correct approach is to contact the study office and submit a schedule conflict request so your case can be reviewed.

> **User:**
> How do I submit the request?

> **Expert:**
> The request requires course-specific information, so you should provide the details of the conflicting courses to ensure accurate evaluation.

> **User:**
> What happens after I submit it?

> **Expert:**
> After submission, the study office reviews the provided information and determines the most appropriate resolution based on scheduling availability.

> **User:**
> Will I be informed about the solution?

> **Expert:**
> Yes. Once the review process is completed, you will receive instructions and can follow the assigned resolution.
